PORTFOLIO

What

This portfolio is a visual presentation of six different texts designed based on the same strand.

Metempsychosis - the transmigration of the soul, especially its reincarnation after death.

The six forms of texts are: poetry, short story, academic, illustrative texts and performance.

Why

I have created this portfolio for two main reasons.

The first is to experiment with the visual design of different text-based media, such as poetry, performance and short stories - all connected to one strand.

The second reason being to explore the concept of metempsychosis - an concept that has long inspired my writing.

How

The strand is connected to the portfolio, and the portfolio does not leave this one static website.

All the texts are combined and integrated into one continuous flow and should be read as such.

The end of every text will unlock the next road at random, so that the flow will not be foreseeable.

My contribution

I wish to learn how to develop showcasing written text, both my own and others, through a website. I believe that the website-format allows for a lot of freedom and creativity. With this portfolio, I wish to contribute six different ways of presenting texts digitally through a website, be it visually or through audio.

Ekphrasis

Lanterns are laid hanging on the trees in brightly colored and illuminating yellow by men in white clothing. Their pointy hats and their haircuts (all the same) frame their faces by the stairs. The blue colored sky, from darker down to lighter. They hang them there, reaching and talking.
Behind them a city emerges, as grand and light as the image – a golden age of creativity, likeminded people gathering, studying, interacting.
I see them there, in their silent utopia, with their lanterns which they hang up high in the trees, but they see me not. I am but a silent spectator of this bizarre show, which to them and to their fellow people seems like any gathering of any sort.
The steps go on for a while, I think. A marble staircase, as tall and grand as the one tree that grows up there. It has taken root in the one place it now stands for such a long time.
Endlessly, time bearing no mark, it stands there, but in my frame, I see only its arms reaching out and being clad in lanterns. One lantern after the other. Big and round, being passed up the staircase from the foreground.
I see them not, the gathering of people – the community, the city that watches these young men work. It is like a play, but more of a ceremony. They stand there, passing the lanterns up and up, as if each lantern resembles a soul. The passing of time, as mirrored by the dark blue sky.
In the corner of my eye the staircase continues and on, until it finally reaches an end. It is part of the mountain on which it was built so long ago. Same as the tree, one part of a larger part. As are the humans with their lanterns which they hand high up. The sky on either side stretches out, but my eyes only see one small part of it. It is dark, and I hardly expect to see the sun, but it is there, the last light of the day or the last year shining away.
The staircases are straight and cover half the image with lines from right to left. In a way, the lines are broken by the lines that make up the young men with their pointy hats. They speak to one another, these young men, not loudly, nor with anger. They seem to be at peace, methodical, and respectful. They know they are being observed, by me, by the others that have come to watch the lamp bearer’s ceremony. There is however a faint manner of small talk in the back, people observing, a child asking the right questions at the wrong time. The roundness of the lanterns strikes me as the one and only shape that is truly a shape, but then I see their hats. They are pointy, like triangles. And they are standing on rectangles that stretch out as stairs. Their haircuts are strangely square as is the sky which is cornered in by the frame. Round blobs of leaves bob around the sky.
Yellow meets white, meets blue, meets green and gray – all under a dark blue sky. The lanterns which they are carrying almost seem to illuminate the scene. Their otherwise white clothes shine with an almost yellowish hue. Shadows seem to be of no existence in this world, instead the hue we see is that which is illuminated by the lanterns. The one thing that is both the focus and the separations are the lanterns. We see them instantly, from a hundred feet away. They are mesmerizing, we follow them from the right to the middle to the top, all the while looking at these young men as they are hanging up the lanterns in the trees. They are, as mentioned also that which separates the scene from the rest. They shield the men and the stairs from the darkness that would otherwise engulf them. They separate them from what would otherwise be a black canvas.
