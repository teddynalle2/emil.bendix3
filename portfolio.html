PORTFOLIO

What

This portfolio is a visual presentation of six different texts designed based on the same strand.

Metempsychosis - the transmigration of the soul, especially its reincarnation after death.

The six forms of texts are: poetry, short story, academic, illustrative texts and performance.

Why

I have created this portfolio for two main reasons.

The first is to experiment with the visual design of different text-based media, such as poetry, performance and short stories - all connected to one strand.

The second reason being to explore the concept of metempsychosis - an concept that has long inspired my writing.

How

The strand is connected to the portfolio, and the portfolio does not leave this one static website.

All the texts are combined and integrated into one continuous flow and should be read as such.

The end of every text will unlock the next road at random, so that the flow will not be foreseeable.

My contribution

I wish to learn how to develop showcasing written text, both my own and others, through a website. I believe that the website-format allows for a lot of freedom and creativity. With this portfolio, I wish to contribute six different ways of presenting texts digitally through a website, be it visually or through audio.

Ekphrasis

Lanterns are laid hanging on the trees in brightly colored and illuminating yellow by men in white clothing. Their pointy hats and their haircuts (all the same) frame their faces by the stairs. The blue colored sky, from darker down to lighter. They hang them there, reaching and talking.
Behind them a city emerges, as grand and light as the image – a golden age of creativity, likeminded people gathering, studying, interacting.
I see them there, in their silent utopia, with their lanterns which they hang up high in the trees, but they see me not. I am but a silent spectator of this bizarre show, which to them and to their fellow people seems like any gathering of any sort.
The steps go on for a while, I think. A marble staircase, as tall and grand as the one tree that grows up there. It has taken root in the one place it now stands for such a long time.
Endlessly, time bearing no mark, it stands there, but in my frame, I see only its arms reaching out and being clad in lanterns. One lantern after the other. Big and round, being passed up the staircase from the foreground.
I see them not, the gathering of people – the community, the city that watches these young men work. It is like a play, but more of a ceremony. They stand there, passing the lanterns up and up, as if each lantern resembles a soul. The passing of time, as mirrored by the dark blue sky.
In the corner of my eye the staircase continues and on, until it finally reaches an end. It is part of the mountain on which it was built so long ago. Same as the tree, one part of a larger part. As are the humans with their lanterns which they hand high up. The sky on either side stretches out, but my eyes only see one small part of it. It is dark, and I hardly expect to see the sun, but it is there, the last light of the day or the last year shining away.
The staircases are straight and cover half the image with lines from right to left. In a way, the lines are broken by the lines that make up the young men with their pointy hats. They speak to one another, these young men, not loudly, nor with anger. They seem to be at peace, methodical, and respectful. They know they are being observed, by me, by the others that have come to watch the lamp bearer’s ceremony. There is however a faint manner of small talk in the back, people observing, a child asking the right questions at the wrong time. The roundness of the lanterns strikes me as the one and only shape that is truly a shape, but then I see their hats. They are pointy, like triangles. And they are standing on rectangles that stretch out as stairs. Their haircuts are strangely square as is the sky which is cornered in by the frame. Round blobs of leaves bob around the sky.
Yellow meets white, meets blue, meets green and gray – all under a dark blue sky. The lanterns which they are carrying almost seem to illuminate the scene. Their otherwise white clothes shine with an almost yellowish hue. Shadows seem to be of no existence in this world, instead the hue we see is that which is illuminated by the lanterns. The one thing that is both the focus and the separations are the lanterns. We see them instantly, from a hundred feet away. They are mesmerizing, we follow them from the right to the middle to the top, all the while looking at these young men as they are hanging up the lanterns in the trees. They are, as mentioned also that which separates the scene from the rest. They shield the men and the stairs from the darkness that would otherwise engulf them. They separate them from what would otherwise be a black canvas.

Dimman över åkern


Volume:
En liten figur sitter under ett mörkt träd. En liten figur vars långa vita svans slingrar sig runt trädstammen. I handen har figuren en själklös blomma som denne hanterar ömt i sina små tassar. I bakgrunden breder en granskog ut sig. Natten tycks ha viljan att sluka den lilla figuren, men månskenet håller en vakande hand över den. Tillsammans med den täta, bleka dimman som sprider ut sig som ett hav ser det ut som att figuren sitter uppspolad på en öde ö. Ändå sitter figuren still under trädet vars utsträckta grenar vinkar mot natthimlen. Där dansar nämligen stjärnorna en hälsning tillbaka. Jag drar ett penseldrag till, nästan i trans, där ett penseldrag nog inte behövs. Varför sitter du där? Känner du dig inte ensam? Allt detta vill jag fråga denna lilla varelse som jag själv skapat. Plötsligt känner jag en mjuk värme, ett tryck på handflatan, när hennes sköra hand greppar min. När jag kollar upp pekar den andra mot fönsterkarmen.

"Nu blommar den," säger mamma. Käraste mamma, där ligger du ju, nedbäddad i sängen med garn i knä. "Sa jag inte att den skulle det?"

"Vadå?" jag ser mig förvirrat omkring. Hon pekar på kaktusen som står vid fönstret. Mycket riktigt har den en blomma. "Vad eget.."

"Jag sa ju att den skulle det," säger hon. Hon hade faktiskt sagt det. Jag hade tänkt på annat när hon sa det, men visst hade hon sagt det. Hon hostar till. Jag tittar på henne och hon förstår vad som utspelar sig bakom mina ögon. "Oroa dig inte, jag mår bra - är det inte eget?" Jag går långsamt upp mot den.

"Jag visste inte - kan kaktusar sånt?" Jag fingrar på blomman, ser de små, nätta bladen. Underligt. Mamma hostar till igen. Jag vänder mig mot henne och på vägen dit stannar mina ögon på det gamla golvuret som långsamt tickar, tickar som om den själv hade ett litet hjärta därinne i dess kärna. Tiden har flugit förbi som den ibland gör. Hög tid för medicin och te bestämmer jag. På vägen till köket lägger jag min handrygg mot hennes, mitt sätt att krama om henne, visa henne min närhet, nu när det gör så ont i hennes kropp. Hennes ögon är fästa på den lilla blomman.

Köket är rent. Rent av min rastlöshet. Rent av alla de timmar jag väntar på att något ska hända, något ska förvärras, något ska - vad var det jag skulle göra? Juste ja, te och medicinen. Te och kanske.. en kokosboll! Mina systrar brukar komma förbi och städa några gånger i veckan. Den yngsta har alltid med sig kokosbollar. Hon brukar blanda i apelsin och dadlar. Jag har egentligen aldrig gillat dadlar.

"Ska den vattnas, tror du?" ropar hon från sovrummet med några dova hostningar som påföljd.

"Ska de ha sånt?" frågar jag lite disträ. Det är konstigt. Samma känsla kommer krypandes likt dimman i min målning. Jag känner mig som den lilla varelsen.. ensam, väntades.. på något -

"Ja, du kan ju försöka med en kopp te," börjar hon retsamt. "Se vad den säger."

"Vadå? Nej, det var inte det jag -"

"En kopp grönt hade inte varit så dåligt!" börjar mamma pipa och ger kaktusen en av hennes berömda röster. "Har du något grönt därinne i köket, mån tro?" Det finns inget annat att göra än att spela med.

"Nej, inget grönt.." jag spelar med så gott jag kan, men jag har alltid känt mig stel vid sådana korta improvisationer. "..men svart?"

"Svart te? Ska du ha död på mig?" Död. Där är det. Det är döden som hänger runt i lägenheten. Jag blir genast svag i kroppen. Döden.

Ökenblomman

A small figure sits beneath a tree emerged in shadows. A small figure, whose long, white tail rests against the tree trunk. The figure holds a small withered flower, which the figure gently cups in their small hands. In the background, a spruce forest spreads out behind the figure and the night seems to have the will to engulf the small figure, but the moonlight shields it. Together with the compact, pale fog that slowly rises out like an endless see, the figure looks stranded where it sits. Without any thought of it, the figure sits in stillness underneath the tree; the tree whose branches slowly waves towards the night sky. There, the stars are dancing a greeting back. Entranced, I slowly add one more brushstroke in a place where a brushstroke is not needed.

Why are you sitting there? Are you not feeling alone? All of this, I wish to ask the small creature that I myself have created. Suddenly, I feel a soft warmth, pressure on my wrist, as her fragile han grips mine. As I look up, I see her other hand point towards the window sill.

“It blooms,” she says. Mother, dearest mother. There you are, lying embedded with yarn in hand. “Didn’t I say so? That it would blossom?”

“What?” I say, and look around in confusion. She’s pointing at the cactus which stands at the window sill. It has indeed a flower. “How odd..” I add.

“I told you it would,” she says - and she really had said it. I had been thinking about something else, but she had said it. She coughs. I look at her, and she understands. Understands what is playing out behind my eyes. “Do not worry, I’m fine - isn’t it odd, though?” I slowly walk towards the cactus.

“I didn’t know.. Are cacti even able to do such a thing?” I touch the small pedals, gently fingering them. Weird. Mother coughs once more and I quickly turn around towards her. On my way there, my eyes grab hold of the large grandfather clock, which is slowly ticking - ticking as if there was a small heart deep inside its core. Time has flown by as it is prone to do. It is high time for medicine and tea. On my way to the kitchen, I gently lay my hand on hers, my way of hugging her now that her body is so sore. Her eyes are locked on to the flower.

The kitchen is clean. Clean because of my restlessness. Clean because of all the hours I sit in wait, sit in case something would worsen, could worsen - what was I doing in the kitchen? Tea and medicine, right. Tea and maybe - a coconut ball. My sisters come by a couple of times a week and help clean and the youngest always brings coconut balls. She usually mixes in oranges and dates. I’ve never really enjoyed the dates.

“It needs water, don’t you think?” she calls from the bedroom with some muffled coughs.

“Do they need it?” I say in disarray. It’s weird. The same feeling is creeping in, as the fog is in my painting. I feel like the small creature. Alone, waiting - waiting for -

“Well, you can always try with a cup of tea,” she teasingly calls. “See what it says!”

“What? No, that’s not what I meant -”

“A cup of tea wouldn’t be so bad!” mother starts piping and gives the cactus one of her oh so famous voices. “Do you have anything green all the way back there in the kitchen?”

“No, nothing green,” I play along as well as can be, but I’ve never really been good at these short improvisations. “.. but black?”

“Black tea? Do you wish me dead?” Dead. There it is. It is death who is creeping around the apartment. All the warmth quickly drains from my body. Death.
